## Patient Profile 1: Cancer Patient

**Name:** Sarah Thompson
**Condition:** Breast Cancer (Stage 2, undergoing chemotherapy)
**Age:** 58
**Allergies:** Penicillin
**Blood Type:** A+

### Health Goals
- Manage treatment side effects
- Maintain strength and energy
- Support immune system
- Improve quality of life

### Diet Plan
**Anti-Cancer Nutrition Plan:**

- Eat small, frequent meals (5-6 times daily) to maintain energy
- Include high-protein foods: eggs, Greek yogurt, lean poultry, fish, beans, nuts
- Colorful vegetables and fruits rich in antioxidants (berries, leafy greens, carrots, tomatoes)
- Whole grains for sustained energy: oatmeal, brown rice, quinoa
- Healthy fats: avocado, olive oil, fatty fish for omega-3s
- Ginger tea for nausea management
- Avoid processed foods, excess sugar, and alcohol
- Stay hydrated with water, herbal teas, and clear broths
- Consider small amounts of dark chocolate for antioxidants and mood

### Exercise Plan
**Gentle Exercise for Cancer Patients:**

- Short walks: Start with 10-15 minutes, 2-3 times daily
- Chair-based stretching exercises: 5-10 minutes morning and evening
- Gentle yoga or tai chi: 20 minutes, 3 times per week
- Deep breathing exercises: 5 minutes, several times daily
- Rest when fatigued - listen to your body
- Gradually increase activity as energy permits
- Avoid overexertion - aim for light to moderate intensity only
- Always warm up slowly and cool down properly

### Medication Schedule
**Cancer Treatment Medications:**

- Chemotherapy: As prescribed by oncologist (track dates and side effects)
- Anti-nausea medication: Take 30 minutes before meals as needed
- Pain management: Take as directed, don't wait for severe pain
- Vitamin D and B12 supplements: With breakfast daily
- Probiotics: To support gut health during treatment
- Keep detailed log of all medications and side effects
- Set phone alarms for medication times
- Never skip or alter doses without consulting oncologist

## Patient Profile 2: Heart Disease Patient

**Name:** Robert Martinez
**Condition:** Coronary Heart Disease, Hypertension, High Cholesterol
**Age:** 65
**Allergies:** None
**Blood Type:** O+

### Health Goals
- Lower blood pressure to target range
- Reduce cholesterol levels
- Maintain healthy weight
- Improve cardiovascular fitness

### Diet Plan
**Heart-Healthy Diet Plan:**

- Mediterranean-style eating: Emphasize plant-based foods
- Omega-3 rich fish: Salmon, mackerel, sardines (2-3 times weekly)
- Abundant vegetables: 5-7 servings daily, especially leafy greens
- Fresh fruits: 3-4 servings daily, focus on berries and citrus
- Whole grains: Oatmeal, brown rice, whole wheat bread
- Nuts and seeds: Small handful of almonds or walnuts daily
- Healthy fats: Olive oil, avocado (limit saturated fats)
- Limit sodium: Under 2000mg daily - avoid processed foods
- Reduce red meat: Choose lean poultry, fish, and plant proteins
- Minimize added sugars and refined carbohydrates
- Stay hydrated with water - limit caffeine and avoid alcohol

### Exercise Plan
**Cardiovascular Exercise Program:**

- Walking: 30 minutes daily, 5 days per week at moderate pace
- Start slowly: Begin with 10-minute sessions if needed
- Swimming or water aerobics: Excellent low-impact option
- Stationary cycling: 20-30 minutes, 3-4 times weekly
- Light strength training: 2 times per week with light weights
- Always warm up: 5 minutes of slow movement before exercise
- Cool down properly: 5 minutes of gentle stretching after
- Monitor heart rate: Stay in safe zone (consult doctor for target)
- Stop if experiencing: Chest pain, severe shortness of breath, dizziness
- Gradually increase duration and intensity over weeks
- Take rest days for recovery

### Medication Schedule
**Heart Medications:**

- Blood pressure medication (Lisinopril): Every morning with breakfast
- Statin for cholesterol (Atorvastatin): Evening dose with dinner
- Baby aspirin: With morning medication
- Blood thinner (if prescribed): Exactly as prescribed, never miss doses
- Record blood pressure: Every morning before medication
- Monitor for side effects: Dizziness, unusual bleeding, muscle pain
- Set daily phone reminders for consistency
- Report any chest discomfort or unusual symptoms immediately

1. **Diagnosis** - Coronary Heart Disease and Hypertension (Date: 2 years ago)
2. **Lab Result** - Cholesterol Panel (Date: 3 months ago)
3. **Treatment** - Medication Adjustment (Date: 2 months ago)
4. **Lab Result** - Blood Pressure Monitoring (Date: 1 week ago)

### Sample Reminders
1. **Medicine** - Blood pressure medication (Daily, 8:00 AM)
2. **Medicine** - Cholesterol medication (Daily, 7:00 PM)
3. **Medicine** - Baby aspirin (Daily, 8:00 AM)
4. **Exercise** - Morning walk (Daily, 7:00 AM)
5. **Appointment** - Cardiology check-up (Monthly, first Monday 10:00 AM)

## Patient Profile 3: Flu Recovery Patient

**Name:** Emily Chen
**Condition:** Influenza A (Active infection, day 7)
**Age:** 42
**Allergies:** Sulfa drugs
**Blood Type:** B+

### Health Goals
- Recover from flu symptoms
- Rebuild strength and energy
- Boost immune system
- Prevent complications

### Diet Plan
**Flu Recovery Nutrition Plan:**

- Hydration is key: Water, herbal teas, clear broths (8-10 glasses daily)
- Chicken soup: Homemade with vegetables for hydration and nutrients
- Vitamin C foods: Oranges, strawberries, bell peppers, kiwi
- Zinc-rich foods: Lean meats, beans, nuts, whole grains
- Ginger tea: Natural anti-inflammatory and nausea relief
- Honey: Add to tea for throat soothing
- Easy-to-digest foods: Bananas, rice, applesauce, toast (BRAT diet)
- Yogurt with probiotics: Support gut health and immunity
- Warm liquids: Help loosen congestion
- Small frequent meals: Easier on upset stomach
- Avoid: Alcohol, caffeine, sugary drinks, heavy fatty foods

### Exercise Plan
**Gentle Recovery Exercise Plan:**

**DURING ACTIVE FLU (Days 1-5):**
- Complete rest is essential - avoid all exercise
- Focus on staying in bed and sleeping
- Gentle stretching only if feeling up to it

**EARLY RECOVERY (Days 6-10):**
- Very light walking: 5-10 minutes around home only
- Gentle stretching: 5 minutes, 1-2 times daily
- Listen to your body - rest if tired

**GRADUAL RETURN (Days 11-14):**
- Slow walks: 10-15 minutes, once or twice daily
- Light household activities as tolerated
- Continue to rest frequently

**FULL RECOVERY (After 2+ weeks):**
- Resume normal activity gradually
- Start with 50% of pre-illness exercise intensity
- Increase slowly over 1-2 weeks to normal levels

### Medication Schedule
**Flu Treatment Guide:**

**OVER-THE-COUNTER:**
- Fever/Pain relief: Acetaminophen (Tylenol) every 4-6 hours as needed
- Decongestant: For nasal congestion (check with doctor first)
- Cough suppressant: For nighttime use if needed
- Never exceed recommended dosages

**PRESCRIPTION:**
- Antiviral medication (Tamiflu): Take within 48 hours of symptom onset
- Complete full course even if feeling better

**SYMPTOM MANAGEMENT:**
- Steam inhalation: 3-4 times daily for congestion
- Saline nasal rinse: Helps clear nasal passages
- Humidifier: Use at night for easier breathing
- Throat lozenges: For sore throat relief

**WARNING SIGNS - SEEK IMMEDIATE CARE:**
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
- Persistent fever over 103°F (39.4°C)
- Severe or persistent vomiting
- Confusion or difficulty staying awake

### Sample Medical Records
1. **Diagnosis** - Influenza A Positive Test (Date: 7 days ago)
2. **Prescription** - Antiviral Medication (Date: 6 days ago)
3. **Treatment** - Symptom Management Plan (Date: 5 days ago)

### Sample Reminders
1. **Medicine** - Antiviral medication (Twice daily, 8:00 AM and 8:00 PM)
2. **Medicine** - Fever reducer as needed (Every 4-6 hours)
3. **Exercise** - Gentle stretching (Daily, 11:00 AM - when feeling better)
4. **Appointment** - Follow-up with primary care (In 2 weeks)

## Admin Account Setup

To access the admin dashboard features:

1. Create an admin account by registering a new user
2. After registration, update the user's role in the database:
   ```sql
   UPDATE profiles SET role = 'admin' WHERE id = 'your-user-id';
   ```
3. Sign out and sign back in to see the admin dashboard
